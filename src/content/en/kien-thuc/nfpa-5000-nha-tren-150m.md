<div class="abox"><p>When a building exceeds the height limits addressed by <strong>QCVN 06:2022/BXD</strong> — generally understood as approximately 150 m — the design team must prepare a technical justification report for the application of a foreign fire code such as <strong>NFPA 5000</strong> or BS 7974. Under Fire Safety Law 55/2024 and Decree 105/2025, the post-inspection model allows greater flexibility — but demands a well-constructed dossier that can withstand scrutiny from the competent authority at any time.</p></div>
<div class="trow"><span class="tag">NFPA 5000</span><span class="tag">Super High-Rise</span><span class="tag">QCVN 06</span><span class="tag">Technical Justification</span><span class="tag">PBD</span></div>

<h2>1. QCVN 06:2022 and its height limitations</h2>
<p>QCVN 06:2022/BXD is the primary Vietnamese national technical regulation for fire safety in buildings and structures. However, the regulation has height-based limitations — super high-rise buildings (generally accepted as above 150 m) fall into a zone that is "not fully covered," particularly with respect to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Refuge floors — location, minimum floor area, and technical requirements</li>
<li>Fire-service lifts and evacuation lifts for persons with disabilities</li>
<li>Emergency communications and incident coordination systems</li>
<li>Smoke control zone-by-zone across pressure-differential compartments</li>
</ul>
<div class="ibox"><strong>📌 Legal basis</strong><p style="font-size:0.85rem;margin:0;color:#555">Under current Vietnamese fire safety regulations, projects may be permitted to apply foreign standards in cases where no equivalent Vietnamese standard exists, or where the foreign standard provides a higher level of safety. A technical justification report is ordinarily required. Please consult current legislation and guidance from the competent authority for your specific project.</p></div>

<h2>2. Why NFPA 5000 is preferred for super high-rise buildings</h2>
<p>NFPA 5000 (Building Construction and Safety Code) is NFPA's comprehensive building code, closely integrated with NFPA 101 (Life Safety Code). The reasons NFPA 5000 is favoured for super high-rise projects in Vietnam:</p>
<table class="ctb"><thead><tr><th>Criterion</th><th>NFPA 5000</th><th>BS 7974</th><th>QCVN 06</th></tr></thead><tbody>
<tr><td>Height coverage</td><td>Unlimited</td><td>Unlimited (PBD)</td><td>Limited ~150 m</td></tr>
<tr><td>Refuge floors</td><td>Specific requirements every 25 storeys</td><td>PBD analysis</td><td>Not fully specified</td></tr>
<tr><td>Evacuation simulation</td><td>Permitted and encouraged</td><td>Core methodology</td><td>Not specified</td></tr>
<tr><td>Evacuation lifts</td><td>Chapter 9 — detailed requirements</td><td>PBD analysis</td><td>Being updated</td></tr>
<tr><td>Integration with Vietnamese PCCC</td><td>High — multiple precedent projects</td><td>Medium</td><td>N/A</td></tr>
</tbody></table>

<h2>3. Technical justification report — step-by-step process</h2>
<div class="step-box"><strong>Step 1 — Gap Analysis</strong><p style="margin-top:0.5rem;font-size:0.9rem;color:#444">Compare each QCVN 06 clause against NFPA 5000: identify provisions where QCVN already applies (use QCVN), provisions where QCVN is absent or insufficient (apply NFPA 5000), and provisions where the two conflict (requiring detailed technical analysis).</p></div>
<div class="step-box"><strong>Step 2 — Design Scenarios</strong><p style="margin-top:0.5rem;font-size:0.9rem;color:#444">Define representative fire scenarios for the building: a fire on a typical floor, a fire in a plant room, a fire in a retail tenancy (if applicable), and a fire in the basement. Each scenario requires FDS fire simulation and Pathfinder egress modelling to demonstrate ASET &gt; RSET.</p></div>
<div class="step-box"><strong>Step 3 — Bilingual Technical Justification Report</strong><p style="margin-top:0.5rem;font-size:0.9rem;color:#444">The report must present: the legal basis, the methodology, simulation results, a comparison against QCVN requirements, and a conclusion on equivalent or superior safety performance. Bilingual format (VI/EN) is required when international consultants are involved.</p></div>
<div class="step-box"><strong>Step 4 — Dossier ready for authority review</strong><p style="margin-top:0.5rem;font-size:0.9rem;color:#444">Under Fire Safety Law 55/2024, the dossier does not need to be submitted before construction begins, but must be ready to present to the fire authority on demand. The dossier includes: calculations, simulation files, design drawings, equipment certificates, and the gap-analysis matrix.</p></div>

<h2>4. Key engineering requirements for buildings above 150 m</h2>
<table class="ctb"><thead><tr><th>Element</th><th>NFPA 5000 requirement</th></tr></thead><tbody>
<tr><td>Refuge floors</td><td>Every 25 storeys; floor area ≥ 0.28 m²/person (NFPA 5000 Ch. 11)</td></tr>
<tr><td>Evacuation lifts</td><td>Fire-rated shaft, dedicated power supply, two-way communication; capacity per NFPA 5000 Ch. 9</td></tr>
<tr><td>Stair pressurisation</td><td>NFPA 92 — 12.5 Pa to 62 Pa; tested with all doors closed and with doors open</td></tr>
<tr><td>Smoke exhaust</td><td>NFPA 92 — zone-by-zone, mechanical, CFD validated where atria or open floor plans are involved</td></tr>
<tr><td>Sprinkler system</td><td>NFPA 13 — high-rise package including seismic restraint</td></tr>
<tr><td>Fire command centre</td><td>Dedicated room; direct voice communication to all areas; fire service access</td></tr>
</tbody></table>

<div class="fqs" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/FAQPage">
<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<div class="fqi" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
<p class="fqq" itemprop="name">Is fire simulation (FDS) mandatory for all buildings over 150 m in Vietnam?</p>
<div class="fqa" itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer"><p itemprop="text">FDS simulation is not explicitly mandated by QCVN 06. However, when a technical justification report is required — as is typically the case for super high-rise buildings that fall outside QCVN 06's scope — fire and smoke simulation (FDS/PyroSim) and egress modelling (Pathfinder) are the established engineering tools used to demonstrate ASET &gt; RSET and support the technical arguments in the dossier.</p></div>
</div>
<div class="fqi" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
<p class="fqq" itemprop="name">Can NFPA 5000 fully replace QCVN 06 for a Vietnamese project?</p>
<div class="fqa" itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer"><p itemprop="text">No. NFPA 5000 is applied as a supplementary reference for areas where QCVN 06 does not provide sufficient guidance — for example, refuge floors and evacuation lift design for super high-rise buildings. Provisions that QCVN 06 covers adequately are still applied as the primary reference. The technical justification report must clearly document which standard governs each design element.</p></div>
