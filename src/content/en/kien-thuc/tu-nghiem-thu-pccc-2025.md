<div class="abox"><p>From 1 July 2025, <strong>Fire Safety Law 55/2024</strong> came into full effect, shifting the compliance model from pre-approval to post-inspection. Project owners, contractors and facility operators now bear direct legal responsibility for fire safety compliance — no prior submission or approval is required before construction or operation begins. The fire authority will inspect after the fact. This article sets out how to prepare a self-certification fire safety dossier that meets the new requirements.</p></div>
<div class="trow"><span class="tag">Fire Safety Law 55/2024</span><span class="tag">Decree 105/2025</span><span class="tag">Post-inspection</span><span class="tag">Self-certification</span><span class="tag">Fire Acceptance 2025</span><span class="tag">NQ 66/2025</span></div>

<h2>1. The fundamental shift — from pre-approval to post-inspection</h2>
<p>Before 1 July 2025, the vast majority of projects were required to go through a "fire safety design review" and a formal "fire safety acceptance" conducted by the Police Fire Service. From 1 July 2025:</p>
<table class="ctb"><thead><tr><th>Aspect</th><th>Before 1 July 2025</th><th>From 1 July 2025</th></tr></thead><tbody>
<tr><td>Model</td><td>Pre-approval — submit dossier, wait for approval</td><td>Post-inspection — self-certify, authority inspects afterwards</td></tr>
<tr><td>Responsibility</td><td>Fire authority confirms each step</td><td>Project owner / contractor bears direct legal responsibility</td></tr>
<tr><td>Terminology</td><td>"Fire safety design review"</td><td>Term no longer used — replaced by "technical assessment" only when required</td></tr>
<tr><td>Dossier submission</td><td>Mandatory before construction / commissioning</td><td>Not required to be submitted in advance — but must be available for inspection at all times</td></tr>
</tbody></table>
<div class="ibox"><strong>⚠️ Important</strong><p style="font-size:0.85rem;margin:0;color:#555">Post-inspection does not mean "nothing needs to be done." The project owner must still ensure full compliance — and if an incident occurs or the fire authority requests an inspection, an incomplete or inadequate dossier will result in more serious penalties than under the previous model.</p></div>

<h2>2. What must the self-certification fire safety dossier contain?</h2>
<p>Under Decree 105/2025, the self-certification dossier must include:</p>

<h3>2.1 Fire safety design documentation</h3>
<ul>
<li>Technical design drawings for all fire safety systems (alarm, suppression, smoke control, egress)</li>
<li>Design specification with calculations based on applicable QCVN / standards</li>
<li>Equipment catalogues and certificates of conformity</li>
<li>Technical justification report if a foreign standard is applied</li>
</ul>

<h3>2.2 Construction and internal acceptance documentation</h3>
<ul>
<li>Construction logs and partial acceptance certificates</li>
<li>System commissioning test results (fire alarm commissioning, sprinkler flush test, smoke control functional test)</li>
<li>Overall acceptance certificate signed by the project owner / main contractor</li>
</ul>

<h3>2.3 Operations and emergency response documentation</h3>
<ul>
<li>Fire Emergency Response Plan — mandatory</li>
<li>Scheduled inspection and maintenance procedures for all fire protection systems</li>
<li>List of on-site fire safety personnel and their training certificates</li>
</ul>

<h2>3. When the authority requests a review</h2>
<p>Under Law 55/2024, the fire authority may require the facility operator or project owner to defend the fire safety design dossier at any time, particularly:</p>
<ul>
<li>Scheduled periodic inspections (annual plan)</li>
<li>Following a fire or explosion incident at the facility</li>
<li>Upon complaint regarding fire safety</li>
<li>At the request of the insurer or a foreign equity investor</li>
</ul>
<p>The dossier should be maintained in a complete and well-organised form — ready to be presented to the competent authority on request.</p>

<h2>4. The role of a fire safety consultant under the post-inspection model</h2>
<p>In the view of many practitioners, the role of an independent fire safety consultant remains essential under post-inspection for the following reasons:</p>
<ul>
<li>The project owner's direct legal exposure may be greater than under the old pre-approval model</li>
<li>The technical dossier must be robust enough to withstand scrutiny by the fire authority, a court, or an insurer — without any prior authority endorsement</li>
<li>Where international standards (NFPA, EN) are applied, independent expertise is required to prepare a technically defensible equivalency report</li>
<li>Post-inspection audits may come without notice; a well-organised, up-to-date dossier is the project owner's primary protection</li>
</ul>

<div class="fqs" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/FAQPage">
<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<div class="fqi" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
<p class="fqq" itemprop="name">Does the post-inspection model apply to existing buildings?</p>
<div class="fqa" itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer"><p itemprop="text">Fire Safety Law 55/2024 and Decree 105/2025 apply to both new projects and existing facilities. Existing buildings that have already obtained fire safety acceptance under the previous model are not required to resubmit — however, any renovation, change of use, or system replacement triggers the obligation to prepare an updated self-certification dossier.</p></div>
</div>
<div class="fqi" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question">
<p class="fqq" itemprop="name">How long must the dossier be retained?</p>
<div class="fqa" itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer"><p itemprop="text">The dossier must be retained for the operational life of the building. Fire safety documentation — including original design drawings, commissioning records, and maintenance logs — must be available for presentation to the fire authority on demand at any time during the facility's operational life.</p></div>
