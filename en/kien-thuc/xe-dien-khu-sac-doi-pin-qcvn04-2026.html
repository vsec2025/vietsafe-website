EV Parking / Charging / Battery-Swap Zones — Fire Safety Comparison

Fire Safety Comparison: EV Parking Zone / EV Charging Zone / Battery-Swap Zone — Circular 31/2026/TT-BXD (Amendment 01:2026 to QCVN 04:2021/BXD)

Regulatory background: Circular 31/2026/TT-BXD issued Amendment 01:2026 to QCVN 04:2021/BXD (National Technical Regulation on Fire Safety for Buildings), introducing dedicated requirements for electric vehicle (EV) parking zones, EV charging zones, and battery-swap stations. This is the first time QCVN explicitly differentiates fire safety requirements based on EV-specific hazards.

Key differences between the three zone types

Criterion EV Parking Zone EV Charging Zone Battery-Swap Station
Fire compartment area (m²)As per QCVN 06≤ 3,000 m² (separate compartment required)≤ 500 m² (separate compartment required)
Automatic suppressionPer occupancy typeRequired (sprinkler)Required (sprinkler or deluge)
Battery capacity limitNo specific limitRegulated per charger typeTotal stored EQ regulated
Ventilation/smoke controlAs per building typeMechanical exhaust requiredMechanical exhaust + gas detection required
Fire detectionConventional alarmHeat + smoke detectionHeat + smoke + off-gas (H₂/CO) detection

Critical hazard — thermal runaway: Lithium-ion batteries can enter uncontrolled self-heating (thermal runaway) without an external ignition source, releasing toxic gases (H₂, CO, HF) before ignition. Amendment 01:2026 requires gas detection systems at battery-swap stations because stored batteries at various states of charge represent the highest thermal runaway risk.

Design implication — charging zones vs. parking zones: A standard EV parking zone where vehicles are not actively charging uses the existing QCVN 06 compartmentation rules for its occupancy type. Once chargers are installed, the zone must be reclassified as an EV charging zone with a maximum 3,000 m² fire compartment and mandatory sprinkler coverage — even if the installed chargers are slow (Level 1/2) AC units.
