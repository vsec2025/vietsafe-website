Fire Safety for Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS)

Fire Safety for Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) — Applying NFPA 855 in Vietnam

Background: Vietnam’s current technical regulations (QCVN) do not yet specifically address battery energy storage systems (BESS). NFPA 855 (Standard for the Installation of Stationary Energy Storage Systems) is therefore the primary reference standard used by VIETSAFE E&C for BESS fire protection design.

Key concepts:

  • Thermal runaway: An uncontrolled self-heating reaction in lithium-ion cells that can lead to fire or explosion even without an external ignition source. Early detection of off-gas (H₂, CO, HF) is critical.
  • Energy quantity (EQ) thresholds: NFPA 855 classifies BESS installations by EQ (kWh), with thresholds determining whether a system is exempt, requires a fire compartment, or must follow full compliance requirements.
  • Suppression: Water-based suppression (sprinkler or fixed deluge) remains the most widely accepted approach. Cooling to prevent thermal propagation between cells is the primary design objective — suppression of flames alone is insufficient.
  • Detection: Aspirating smoke detectors (ASD/VESDA) and combustible/toxic gas detectors (H₂, CO, HF) for very early warning, combined with conventional heat and smoke detection.
  • Compartmentation: Fire-rated separation, explosion relief venting, and controlled drainage are required for indoor BESS rooms per NFPA 855 §4.3 and Table 4.3.2.

Vietnamese regulatory gap: QCVN 06:2022/BXD covers general fire protection for buildings but does not address BESS. Applying NFPA 855 in Vietnam requires a technical justification dossier submitted to the fire authority (Cảnh sát PCCC) for approval under Art. 7 of Circular 06/2023/TT-BCA.
