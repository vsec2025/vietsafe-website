NFPA 5000 for Super High-Rise Buildings — Applying Foreign Fire Codes for Projects Above 150 m

NFPA 5000 — Technical Justification for Super High-Rise Projects in Vietnam

When a building exceeds the height limits addressed by QCVN 06:2022/BXD — generally understood as approximately 150 m — the design team must prepare a technical justification report for the application of a foreign fire code such as NFPA 5000 or BS 7974. Under Fire Safety Law 55/2024 and Decree 105/2025, the post-inspection model allows greater flexibility — but demands a well-constructed dossier that can withstand scrutiny from the competent authority at any time.

1. QCVN 06:2022 and its height limitations

QCVN 06:2022/BXD is the primary Vietnamese national technical regulation for fire safety in buildings and structures. However, the regulation has height-based limitations — super high-rise buildings (generally accepted as above 150 m) fall into a zone that is "not fully covered," particularly with respect to:

  • Refuge floors — location, minimum floor area, and technical requirements
  • Fire-service lifts and evacuation lifts for persons with disabilities
  • Emergency communications and incident coordination systems
  • Smoke control zone-by-zone across pressure-differential compartments
📌 Legal basis

Under current Vietnamese fire safety regulations, projects may be permitted to apply foreign standards in cases where no equivalent Vietnamese standard exists, or where the foreign standard provides a higher level of safety. A technical justification report is ordinarily required. Please consult current legislation and guidance from the competent authority for your specific project.

2. Why NFPA 5000 is preferred for super high-rise buildings

NFPA 5000 (Building Construction and Safety Code) is NFPA's comprehensive building code, closely integrated with NFPA 101 (Life Safety Code). The reasons NFPA 5000 is favoured for super high-rise projects in Vietnam:

3. Technical justification report — step-by-step process

Step 1 — Gap Analysis

Compare each QCVN 06 clause against NFPA 5000: identify provisions where QCVN already applies (use QCVN), provisions where QCVN is absent or insufficient (apply NFPA 5000), and provisions where the two conflict (requiring detailed technical analysis).

Step 2 — Design Scenarios

Define representative fire scenarios for the building: a fire on a typical floor, a fire in a plant room, a fire in a retail tenancy (if applicable), and a fire in the basement. Each scenario requires FDS fire simulation and Pathfinder egress modelling to demonstrate ASET > RSET.

Step 3 — Bilingual Technical Justification Report

The report must present: the legal basis, the methodology, simulation results, a comparison against QCVN requirements, and a conclusion on equivalent or superior safety performance. Bilingual format (VI/EN) is required when international consultants are involved.

Step 4 — Dossier ready for authority review

Under Fire Safety Law 55/2024, the dossier does not need to be submitted before construction begins, but must be ready to present to the fire authority on demand. The dossier includes: calculations, simulation files, design drawings, equipment certificates, and the gap-analysis matrix.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is fire simulation (FDS) mandatory for all buildings over 150 m in Vietnam?

FDS simulation is not explicitly mandated by QCVN 06. However, when a technical justification report is required — as is typically the case for super high-rise buildings that fall outside QCVN 06's scope — fire and smoke simulation (FDS/PyroSim) and egress modelling (Pathfinder) are the established engineering tools used to demonstrate ASET > RSET and support the technical arguments in the dossier.

Can NFPA 5000 fully replace QCVN 06 for a Vietnamese project?

No. NFPA 5000 is applied as a supplementary reference for areas where QCVN 06 does not provide sufficient guidance — for example, refuge floors and evacuation lift design for super high-rise buildings. Provisions that QCVN 06 covers adequately are still applied as the primary reference. The technical justification report must clearly document which standard governs each design element.
