NFPA 5000 for Super High-Rise Buildings over 150 m in Vietnam

NFPA 5000 for Super High-Rise Buildings over 150 m in Vietnam

Regulatory gap: Vietnam’s QCVN 06:2022/BXD provides fire safety requirements for high-rise buildings up to approximately 150 m. For towers exceeding this height, the regulation is silent — creating a compliance gap that must be addressed through an alternative solution process using NFPA 5000 (Building Construction and Safety Code).

Process for applying NFPA 5000 in Vietnam:

  1. Gap analysis: A clause-by-clause comparison of QCVN 06 and NFPA 5000, identifying where the Vietnamese code is silent or less stringent.
  2. Technical justification dossier: A report demonstrating that the NFPA 5000-based design achieves an equivalent or higher level of fire safety than QCVN 06 would provide if it were applicable.
  3. Authority approval: Submission to the Ministry of Construction (Bộ Xây dựng) for approval of the alternative code application per Art. 7, Circular 06/2023/TT-BCA.

Key NFPA 5000 requirements for super high-rise buildings:

  • Refuge floors: Required at intervals not exceeding 30 storeys; must be compartmented and pressurised.
  • Smoke control: Stairwell and lift shaft pressurisation; mechanical smoke exhaust for corridors and atriums.
  • Egress: ASET/RSET analysis using simulation tools (Pathfinder, FDS) to verify that Available Safe Egress Time exceeds Required Safe Egress Time for all occupants.
  • Structural fire resistance: Fire resistance ratings per NFPA 5000 Chapter 7; fireproofing for structural steel may differ from QCVN requirements.
